## Structured accent database sources used

### George Mason University Speech Accent Archive

The Speech Accent Archive provides standardized recordings of the same elicitation paragraph, making it useful for comparing pronunciation patterns across accents. It includes audio, phonetic/transcription-style resources, and speaker metadata. Creative Commons previously described the archive as CC-licensed, but you should verify current rights/terms before using these clips commercially or redistributing them.

## Research-only / listen-only sources

### IDEA — International Dialects of English Archive

IDEA is extremely useful for authentic dialect research, including English regional dialects and English spoken with other-language accents. However, IDEA explicitly says it retains copyright and that sound files may not be distributed without express written consent, often with a fee. For that reason, I included IDEA pages only as listening/research links and did not include their audio files in the downloader.

Used link-only references:
- West Country / Southwest England: IDEA England 35
- Louisiana 6: Cajun/French roots, Plaquemine
- Louisiana 10: New Orleans with Cajun/country sounds in the mix

### University of Hawai‘i / Sato Center — Hawai‘i Creole English

Used as a free online listening/reference link for Hawai‘i Creole English / Hawai‘i Pidgin-influenced English. The exact redistribution license was not clearly verified in accessible results, so it is included as a source-page link, not as downloadable packaged audio.

## Practical advice

For game voice/accent design work:

1. Use the LibriVox public-domain clips as the safest downloadable references.
2. Use Speech Accent Archive for comparison and phonetic study; verify current reuse terms before redistributing.
3. Use IDEA and University of Hawai‘i links for listening/research only unless you obtain explicit permission.
4. Do not clone any real speaker’s voice. Use clips as accent references for rhythm, vowel/consonant tendencies, and prosody, not as identity replication.
